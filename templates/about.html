Who are you?

My name is Chris Allison. I'm an executive in the federal government of Canada and a developer / data person. I believe data is one of our best tools to identify and eliminate bias in our organizations. I'm working on this project as a way to test the hypothesis and work towards a more equitable and inclusive future.

Why does this project matter?

The current demographic surveys in use in the Government of Canada artificially divide people into (often binary) categories:

  • Visible Minority (or not)
  • Person with a disability (or not)
  • Male, female or other

This practice:

  • Doesn’t represent the complexity of experience that each person brings to the table
  • Does not explain how different people’s experiences of this part of their lives may differ greatly
  • Does not track or infer the impact of what is assumed to be the “norm” - white, cis, abled, male

This project is an attempt to build a data model and lens through which to:

  • Better model lived experience as it relates to and impacts our work
  • Build empathy and compassion for ourselves and others
  • Make privilege and inclusiveness visible in our organizations so we can work to balance the scales

How will this project make things better?

This MVP is designed to give participants the control to define their lived experiences as they perceive them, one by one.

By combining, or layering experiences, we can provide a more holistic understanding of a person’s lived experience in their own words.

By combining lived experiences into a graph that links common concepts like “manager”, “woman”, “black”, “mother”, “father” or “millennial”, we can see how experiences interact with each other and develop a better understanding of how our workplaces impact our people.

By calling out and making explicit the “normal”, we can identify systemic privilege and bias in our organizations.

What is this work based on?

The structure of the survey is based on the Person-Role-System, a framework for understanding dynamic relationships between people in the roles and systems in which they function.

You can learn about this framework and how it can contribute to culture change needed for equitable organizations below.

Leading for Results: The Person-Role-System Framework

More information can also be found in the excellent blog article by @Living_Cities.

Is it finished?

No.

This work is an MVP (minimal viable product) to test the survey, data and design methodology.

There is a great deal of testing and questions to ask before I would put this in front of our employees.

But it is a start and I’d love your feedback.

The code and issues can be created on Github.

The survey asks personal questions. How will you protect my information?

Intersections has been designed to ensure your privacy.

No identifying information is stored as part of the survey. When you finish the survey, you will be provided with a code so you can access your profile.

You are the only person who will receive this code and without the code, no-one can view your profile.
